# Governance Model for EduLibra

## Overview
EduLibra utilizes an Agile Scrum framework to ensure efficient project management and delivery. This model emphasizes iterative progress, regular stakeholder engagement, and continuous feedback.

## Decision-Making Structure

### Roles and Responsibilities
- **Product Owner:** Represents the stakeholders and prioritizes the backlog to maximize project value.
- **Scrum Master:** Facilitates Scrum processes, removes impediments, and ensures team productivity.
- **Development Team:** Responsible for delivering potentially shippable increments of the product at the end of each sprint.
- **Project Manager:** Oversees project execution, ensures alignment with business objectives, and manages stakeholder communications.

### Governance Bodies
1. **Steering Committee:** Comprised of senior stakeholders who provide strategic direction and resolve escalated issues.
   - Meets bi-monthly to review progress and align on strategic decisions.

2. **Project Core Team:** Includes the Product Owner, Scrum Master, Development Team Lead, and Project Manager.
   - Meets weekly to review sprint progress and plan upcoming activities.

3. **Quality Assurance Board:** Focuses on maintaining high-quality standards and ensuring compliance with ISTQB testing principles.
   - Conducts bi-weekly reviews of test plans, execution, and defect management processes.

## Change Management Process
- Changes are captured in Redmine and evaluated based on impact and priority.
- Minor changes are approved by the Product Owner.
- Major changes requiring additional resources or affecting timeline are escalated to the Steering Committee.

## Risk Management
- Risks are identified and documented in a risk register with mitigation actions.
- Regular risk assessment reviews are conducted by the Project Core Team.

## Compliance and Audits
- Regular audits and compliance checks are conducted to ensure adherence to Indian data protection laws and educational standards.
